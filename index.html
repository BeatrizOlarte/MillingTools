Abstract

This ontology describes and models solid end milling tools, with the aim of creating interoperability across different vendors. Our ontology models the solid end mills, linking geometry, cutting parameters and milling tasks.This ontology allows mapping different cutting tool manufacturers information for solid end mills, enabling interoperability. An application as cutting tool recommendation engine is being developed. The ontology reuses previous ontologies to enhance the resusability principle. InVor ontology was replicated from UML diagram as it was not possible to find it. However, componency from Ontology Design Patterns was downloaded from w3c as well as the and foaf vocabulary (see References).

KG4CUT - Knowledge Graph for Cutting Tools: Description back to ToC

Ontology version 1.3.0 encompasses a comprehensive set of core classes that are fundamental to the domain of machining. It addresses the intricate and highly interdependent nature of machining operationsparticularly the critical decision-making processes involved in the selection of cutting tools. These decisions are influenced by numerous factors, including the wide variety of tool manufacturers, the specific requirements of the geometry to be machined, the properties of the workpiece material, and the capabilities of the machine tool itself. Furthermore, the complexity increases significantly in the context of large-scale or sophisticated milling tasks. The ontology is designed to streamline and support this multifaceted decision-making process by formally capturing domain knowledge, enabling greater interoperability, consistency, and automation in machining-related applications.
